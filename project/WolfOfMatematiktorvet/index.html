Project Motivation
We were able to extract about 50,000 company articles from Wikipedia. Based on the knowledge we
gained in the DTU course 02805 Social Graphs and Interactions, we wanted to gain more insights into
the network of companies with the help of graph theory.

Each company article contains a template to extract additional company properties including type,
industry, location, key people and more, in total up to 30 attributes. Download the raw data (Python
dictionary as pickle file) to conduct some research yourself.

Running several network analyses, the goal is to find interesting facts about communities, most central
companies and so on. After, a sentiment analysis will be conducted to identify trends about key
companies from tweets on Twitter.

Preliminary Analysis:
History of Companies

The preliminary analysis contains no network analysis, but is a simple overview of the extracted data. The below plot is the distribution of founded/defunct companies, extracted from the data source, after the year 1800.

Generally, the amount of companies increases gradually along the time. Especially after the millennium there were many foundations. Many companies were dissolved after the global financial crisis in 2008. There is a valley for foundations in the 1940s which could be caused by the second world war. After the war years there is a big increase possibly influenced by the Marshall Plan in Western Europe. In the last 3 years from now the numbers overall drop a bit but that could very well be, because lately founded companies are not as successful yet to create an article on Wikipedia.


History of Industries

The plot shows the distribution of the 5 industries with the highest amount of founded or defunct companies after the year 1800.

The automobile industry remained silent and was suddenly boosted rapidly after 1900. Several historical events took place: Prior to 1900, the automobile was really a novelty item, but in 1908, Ford created the Model T, which became the first car aggressively marketed to the average family. Commercialization began, but the number dropped in the next decade again because of the great depression in 1929, but was accelerated again soon after [1].

The number of a high defunct rate correlates with the number of low foundation rate in the automobile industry in 1900-1920. Car manufacturing at that time is comparable to internet entrepreneurship nowadays. But the high competition and economy crises led to a demise of companies in the car industry.

Same as for the automobile industry can be said for the aerospace industry. Its development is a byproduct of the world wars and the cold war afterwards. Rocket engines were also used for missiles and aircrafts. Many milestones like the space shuttle Columbia, the space station "Mir" and "ISS" were made in the 80s. After space commercialization and space tourism and nowadays projects like SpaceX are gaining ground [2]. However, due to the immature technologies, industrial uncertainty and high cost in R&D, many companies failed to keep up with the speed and thus ended to be defunct.

Hidden Communities


Companies today are usually categorized into branches, industries or type. However, hidden communities might actually provide a more truthful view of the companies.

The Louvain Algorithm as well as the Infomap algorithm is used to identify and extract communities. Communities can be any sort of branches generated between companies. As the Louvain Algorithm only applies to undirected graphs, the Infomap algorithm will be used as well for even better community detection, as it can also be run on directed graphs.

Around 60 hidden communities were found by the Louvian Algorithm, while the Infomap algorithm were able to detect 2436 for the directed graph and 2095 for the undirected.

Closeness centrality is a measure that wasn't introduced in the course curriculum but we wanted to know more about it. Closeness centrality is sum of the shortest path lengths between the node and all other nodes. Thus the node that's closest to all others is the most central. NetworkX also normalizes the closeness by the sum of minimum possible distances.

Top 5 Largest Communities






Nodes from the largest communities are added to a directed graph, which constitutes 3273 nodes and 3280 edges. At the same time, the Weakly Connected Component network were extracted. This network contains 1213 nodes and 3233 edges.

The Katz centrality is similar to eigenvector centrality as it generalizes it. A practical problem with eigenvector centrality is that it works well only if the graph is (strongly) connected. The problem with eigenvector centrality is nodes with no incoming edges have, by definition, a null eigenvector centrality score, and so have nodes that are pointed to by only nodes with a null centrality score [4]. Thus Katz suggested that every node gets a small centrality value now nodes with few links may still have high centrality if the linkers have a large centrality. High centrality nodes stay unaffected by the centrality of the linkers.

Sentiment Analysis:
Wordclouds & TF-IDF
Based on the network analysis, select companies were chosen to conduct a sentiment analysis on. Tweets about these companies were extracted to identify whether these companies still suffered bad press due to scandals. One such example is the scandal involving a worldwide recall of the Samsung Galaxy Note 7 due to explosions.

Tweets are collected and converted to a list of words that are needed to generate the TF-IDF. TF-IDF is a simple weighting scheme to find important words within a category. These can be visualized in word or tag clouds where words with higher tf–idf value appear bigger and thus are more important in the document. Our goal is to create that kind of wordcloud.




Word suchs as 'destroy', 'explosion', 'risk', 'flame' and 'battery' can all be found in the wordcloud. They are not the main subject but it is definitely something people still talk about. It also seems many people compare Samsung to Apple and the iPhone. Another dominating word is "grilling" which is related to a very recent scandal involving Samsung. Some news headlines below.
