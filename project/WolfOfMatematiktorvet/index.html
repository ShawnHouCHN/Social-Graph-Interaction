Project Motivation
Wikipedia contains more than 50,000 notable companies. The data is gathered from articles about companies,
including type, industry, location and more. Running several network analyses, the goal is to find interesting
facts like hidden communities that are not only formed by type, industry or country. At the same time, a sentiment
analysis will be conducted to identify trends about key companies.

Rediscover the world.
By analyzing the companies on Wikipedia, 56898 nodes
and 1055939 edges were identified.

Surprising interconnections between these companies
where revealed. Which company is the biggest? Who is
the most connected? If one giant connected component
were to collapse, would the rest follow?

Find out more below.

Preliminary Analysis:
History of Companies

The preliminary analysis contains no network analysis but is simply an overview of the extracted data. The below plot is the distribution of founded/defunct companies, extracted from the data source, after the year 1800.

Generally, the amount of companies increases gradually along the time. Especially after the millennium there were many foundations. Many companies were dissolved after the global financial crisis in 2008. In the last 3 years the numbers all drop a bit but that could very well be because lately founded companies are not as successful yet to create an article on Wikipedia.


History of Industries

The plot is the distribution of the top 5 industries with the highest amount of founded/defunct companies after the year 1800. All industries have been analysed, but only the top 5 are shown in the plot for clearer illustrations.

  • The automobile industry remained silent and was suddenly boosted rapidly after 1900. Several historical events took place. Prior to 1900, the automobile was really a novelty item, but in 1908, Ford created the Model T, which became the first car aggressively marketed to the average family. Commercialization began, but the number dropped in the next decade again because of the great depression in 1929 but accelerated again soon after [1].

  • The software industry really starts around the 80s with the emergence of computer technology.

  • The communication industry also rocketed similar to software companies with the emergence of the computer and internet.

  • The amount of companies founded in retail and finance increased steadily along the time.

  • The financial industry had a stable increasing rate/trend of dissolutions until 1980-2000 where it made an increase of dissolutions of over 100%. Today the amount of defunct financial companies remains twice as high as compared to the 1960-1980 period.

  • The number of high defunct rate correlates with the number of low foundation rate in the automobile industry in 1900-1920. Car manufacturing at that time is comparable to internet entrepreneurialism nowadays. But the high competition and economy crises led to a demise of companies in the car industry.

  • Same can be said for the aerospace industry. The development of aerospace industry is a byproduct of the world wars and the cold war afterwards. At the beginning of 80s, the space shuttle Columbia launched, the start of regular manned access to orbital space. After Space commercialization and space tourism and nowadays projects like SpaceX are gaining ground [2]. However, due to the immature technologies, industrial uncertainty and high cost in R&D, many companies failed to keep up with the speed thus ended to be defunct.

  • The amount of companies defunct in the retail and manufacturing industry generally complies to the incremental tendency along time.


Global Scatter of Companies

The heat maps below show how distribution of the companies are spread across the world. The data collected from Wikipedia mostly represents the US and Europe, however still covers most of the world.

  • Global
  • Europe
  • United States
  • Asia

The global hot spots appear mostly in east and west coasts of the United States as well as western Europe.

In Europe the companies are much more distributed and not only in specific regions although London and Paris stick out as hot spots.

In the US, the hot spots appear in New York, San Francisco and Los Angeles. Besides, the hotspot appeared at the centroid of the map stands for those companies that have their locations registered as "USA" rather than specific cities.

In Asia there is not as many company distributions also because there is probably not as much data.
